Egyptian Museum

The Egyptian Museum in Cairo (EMC) is the oldest archaeological museum in the Middle East, housing over 170,000 artefacts. It has the largest collection of Pharaonic antiquities in the world. The Museum’s exhibits span the Pre-Dynastic Period till the Graeco-Roman Era (c. 5500 BC - AD 364).

City of David / Hezekiah's Tunnel

Hezekiah's Tunnel is an ancient water tunnel in Jerusalem, carved by King Hezekiah of Judah around 700 BC. It was built to divert water from the Gihon Spring inside the city, safeguarding Jerusalem from potential enemies during sieges. The tunnel is a marvel of ancient engineering, connecting the spring to the Pool of Siloam within the city walls..
